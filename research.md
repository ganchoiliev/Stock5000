# Research: Effective Stock Tracking Apps & Dashboards

A review of the most popular and effective stock portfolio trackers (like Portfolio Pilot, Strabo, Ziggma, Stock Analysis, etc.) reveals several key commonalities in how they present and manage data. 

## What Top Dashboards Have in Common

1. **Portfolio Aggregation & Multi-Account Linking**
   The ability to sync data automatically from various brokerages, retirement accounts, and crypto wallets into one unified view.
   
2. **Clear, Real-time (or Near Real-time) Updates**
   Investors need immediate clarity on how their assets are performing right now, rather than waiting for end-of-day summaries.

3. **Advanced Performance Analytics & Benchmarking**
   Top dashboards don't just show total returns. They provide time-weighted and money-weighted returns, and critically, allow users to benchmark their performance against major indices (like the S&P 500 or Nasdaq).

4. **Visual Asset Allocation & Diversification Insights**
   Visual tools (donut charts, tree maps) that immediately highlight a user's exposure by sector, geography, or asset class, helping them understand their risk profile.

5. **Dividend & Income Tracking**
   Dedicated sections for forecasting future income streams, tracking past dividend payments, and analyzing dividend yields.

6. **Customizable Watchlists & Smart Alerts**
   Users can personalize notifications for price movements, earnings reports, or breaking news on specific holdings or watchlist items.

7. **Clean, Intuitive UX/UI**
   Despite processing vast amounts of complex financial data, the best platforms use minimalist, highly organized interfaces that prioritize legibility over clutter.

## The Most Important Information to Have

If you are building a stock tracking dashboard or MVP, the hierarchy of information should prioritize the following:

### 1. The "Headline" Metrics (Top of Page)
- **Total Portfolio Value**: The absolute current worth of all linked assets.
- **Daily & All-Time Return**: Both absolute dollar amount ($) and percentage (%) change.

### 2. High-Level Visualizations
- **Performance Chart**: A line chart showing portfolio growth over various timeframes (1D, 1W, 1M, 1Y, ALL) compared to a benchmark index.
- **Allocation Breakdown**: A quick visual representation of how the portfolio is divided (e.g., 60% Tech, 20% Healthcare, 20% Cash).

### 3. Individual Asset Details (The Table or Tiles)
For each holding, the dashboard MUST display:
- **Ticker Symbol / Company Name**
- **Current Price** & **Daily Change (%)**
- **Total Shares Owned**
- **Overall Position Value**
- **Total Gain/Loss (%)** on that specific asset
- **Average Cost Basis** (What they paid for it)

### 4. Actionable Alerts & Watchlist
- A sidebar or dedicated section showing tickers the user wants to monitor, along with any urgent alerts (e.g., "AAPL earnings call today" or "TSLA down 5%").

### Summary
The most effective dashboards strike a balance between providing **instant, at-a-glance health metrics** of an entire portfolio and offering **deep-dive analytics** for individual assets when the user wants to drill down.
